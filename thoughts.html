Reflections on Media Categorization

Identified Common Themes

In reviewing the assignments of my peers, I've identified several common themes in how we categorize and interact with media. These insights have prompted me to reconsider my own media categorization framework.

  • Theme 1: Fast media vs. Traditional Media - Many students, including Finn and Uma , discussed the diffuciulty of using traditional forms of media such as text or books and rather rely of video content or shorter forms of content to digest.
  • Theme 2: Supported Systems - These students disccuss simmilarities between their preffered form of media such as Mateo who liked viewing films and Nikki who enjoys playing video games, both highlight how their experieces were enhanced by supporting system such as a community or technological support play crucial roles in maximizing the enjoyment.
  • Theme 3: Overconsumption - The overconsumption of media was discussed Akhil and Neb, sharing how easy it was to become stuck in a loop while using media.

Revisions to My Media Categorization

After relfecting on the common themes I read over, I propose certain revisions to my media categorization:

  • I would also expand certian media by the supported systems acknowledging how films and video games benefit from the enahncemens provided by a community and technological support.
